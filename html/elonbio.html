Biography of Elon Musk

Early Life

Elon Musk was born on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa. He developed an early interest in computing and technology, teaching himself programming at the age of 10. He moved to Canada at age 17 before eventually attending the University of Pennsylvania in the United States.

Career

Musk co-founded Zip2, an online city guide software, which was later sold to Compaq for nearly $300 million. He then started X.com, an online payment company, which eventually became PayPal after a merger. In 2002, Musk founded SpaceX, aiming to make space exploration more affordable. He joined Tesla Motors in 2004, helping revolutionize the electric vehicle industry, and later founded other ventures like SolarCity, Neuralink, and The Boring Company.

Achievements

Elon Musk is known for his contributions to space exploration and sustainable energy. SpaceX became the first private company to send astronauts to the International Space Station, while Tesla has become a leader in electric vehicles. Musk has also been influential in developing renewable energy solutions, tunneling technology, and advancements in brain-machine interfaces.
